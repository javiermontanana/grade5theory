So far, we have only seen intervals up to an octave. In this section we will learn how to identify any interval bigger than an octave. Intervals bigger than an octave are called compound intervals. Intervals up to an octave are called simple intervals.

number

The interval number works as you might expect. We still just count the number of stave positions.

This is a 9th This is a 10th

But there is another way to describe the number of these intervals.

This is a compound 2nd This is a compound 3rd

With use the word to mean "plus an octave."

Be careful not to get confused by the numbers. A second plus an octave is a ninth, even though 2+8=10. You can't just add the numbers together or you'll be over by one! Instead, count the steps.

quality

The interval quality of a compound interval is the same as that of it's corresponding simple interval. This means that we can just shrink the interval by a (perfect) octave and find the quality of that interval.
